# Getting started

RaioPDF opens a PDF from your computer and gives you what you need to read,
organize, edit, and prepare it — all on your own machine, with no account to
create.

## Open a PDF

There are two ways:

1. **Drag a PDF onto the window.**
2. Click **Open a PDF** and choose one from your computer.

The start screen shows "Open a PDF to get started." Once you're working, the
**Open** button in the toolbar brings up another file at any time.

If a file won't open, RaioPDF offers to **repair** it — some PDFs are built in
unusual ways, and a repair pass usually fixes them.

## Find your way around

- The **toolbar** across the top handles the basics: open, save, print, moving
  page to page, zoom, and search.
- The **Tools** panel on the left is grouped into **Edit**, **Annotate**,
  **Organize**, and **Legal**, with **Make Searchable (OCR)** on its own.
  **Edit** changes the document's real content (Find & Replace, page numbers,
  watermarks); **Annotate** layers comments and markup on top of it.
- **Select** is your normal reading mode — the plain pointer. When you're done
  with any tool, click it again or press **Esc** to return to Select.

## Saving your work

Tools change the document you have open; nothing is written to your disk until
you **Save**. Use **Save As** to write the changed version to a new file and
keep the original untouched.
